Pork Shank or "Eisbein" or "Schweinshaxe"

Eisbein is a traditional German dish of juicy, tender pork shank with the crispiest, crackly skin.

Eisbein is a perfectly juicy pork shank with a crisp and crackle of skin as you bite through. Pair with sauerkraut and you have the perfect dish. It is absolutely WUNDERBAR!

Ingredients

  • 2 - 2.5lbs Pork shank (also referred to as Pork knuckle)
  • 6 Garlic cloves
  • 2 tbsp Olive oil
  • 2 tbsp Vinegar
  • 2 tsp Salt (I prefer coarse sea salt)
  • 1 tsp Black pepper
  • 1 tsp Juniper berries
  • 1 tsp Caraway seeds
  • 1 tsp Fennel seeds

Preperation

  1. Start by pricking the skin all over using a small pin. Do not pierce through the fat. We're doing this for the skin to crisp, we don't want meat juices flowing through.
  2. Brush 1 tsp of the vinegar all over the flesh of the pork shank. Avoid getting vinegar on the skin or peeling the skin back.
  3. Make a small shallow incision in the pork flesh to insert each garlic clove.
  4. Cover pork skin with olive oil. Then rub the seasoning of the salt, black pepper, juniper berries, caraway seeds, and fennel seeds all over the flesh and skin.
  5. Best you can, pull the skin down to stretch it. When in place, use 2 metal scewars at the base (~1") and pierce through the pork shank in a 'X' fashion. This will hold the skin stretched down which will ensure the skin is nice and crispy.
  6. If you are able place on a plate and put in the refridgerator over night to marinate.

Cooking Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F
  2. You will need an oven safe pan that allows room or has the ability to accomedate a rack. Place the pork shank on top of the rack. If adding vegetables or sauerkraut, place them under the rack below the pork shank.
  3. Brush the remaining 1 tsp of vinegar on the skin.
  4. Leave in the oven for 30 minutes, turning half way through.
  5. Remove from oven and let rest for 15 minutes before serving.
